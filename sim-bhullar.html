A career in High School

Sim Bhullar attended The Kiski School in Saltsburg, Pennsylvania, during the 2009-10 school year. He grew up in Brampton, Toronto, Ontario, and attended Father Henry Carr Catholic Secondary School in Etobicoke before moving to Saltsburg.

College Life

Bhullar began his playing career at Xavier University in Cincinnati, Ohio, but left to play for the New Mexico State Aggies in August 2011. As a result, he dropped out of Xavier because he realized he was not qualified to play for the university. He could not play until the 2012-13 season, however, because the NCAA revoked his eligibility, waived him, and rejected his school’s inducement. Later in his rookie season, he averaged 10.1 points, 6.7 rebounds, and 2.4 blocks in 24.4 minutes per game. In his sophomore season, he averaged 10.4 points, 7.8 rebounds, and 3.4 blocks per game.

As a result, he was named two-time WAC tournament MVP in two consecutive years, 2013 and 2014, and he also helped New Mexico State reach the NCAA tournament. As a result, he declared for the NBA draft in April 2014, foregoing his final two years of college eligibility.

Professional Development

After going undrafted in the 2014 NBA draft, Sim Bhullar joined the Sacramento Kings for the 2014 NBA Summer League. As a result, he signed with the Kings in August 2014, becoming the NBA’s first player of Indian descent. He was later waived by the Kings on October 19 after appearing in two preseason games with them.

In addition, in November 2014, he was acquired by the Kings’ NBA Development League affiliate team, the Reno Bighorns. As a result, he made his debut for the team in December 2014, recording four points, eight rebounds, and six blocks against the Los Angeles D-Fenders.
